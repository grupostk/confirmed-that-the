Yesterday the development Minister Suhaimi Gafar confirmed that the country’s biggest project will be open in 2020

December 4, 2019 0 By Edward Ankney

Southeast Asia’s soon to be the longest oversea bridge —  The Temburong Bridge is now 75% nearing completion and on track to meet its November 2019 deadline.

The Temburong Bridge Project sites in Kota Batu, Brunei-Muara and Labu Estate and Temburong were visited by H.R.H. Prince Haji Al-Muhtadee Billah Crown Prince and Chief Minister at the Prime Minister’s Office. His delegation examined the single tower cable-stayed bridge that connects the northern and southern ramps of Jalan Kota Batu and the sub-water viaducts that cross Brunei. The Minister discussed that the authorities must ensure that contractors comply with all safety standards and that they do not significantly increase office hours without authorization.

The $1.6 billion plan is scheduled to be open to the public in 2020 after the completion of the patchwork, which will take three to five months, according to Suhaimi Gafar, Minister of Development of Brunei.

The bridge is one of the largest public infrastructure projects in Brunei, linking Brunei-Mura to Temburong, and is expected to reduce travel time between the two districts by enabling commuters to bypass Limbang, Malaysia.

The bridge project started in 2009 with the release of a proposal by the Ministry of Development to determine the feasibility of the bridge. The construction of a billion-dollar bridge began in 2014 after the government awarded contracts to Daelim, a South Korean company, and China State Building Engineering Corporation.
